Welcome to MS DCS Training Study Guide

Introduction

Much of what’s going on inside the DCS Operator’s mind is recalling. Recalling a lot of information. This information was stored over hundreds of hours of sitting in the DCS chair absorbing data. (For cases that has never happened before, a deliberation with other Operators will be conducted to come up with an appropriate action. Recalling to deliberate is of course part of the recall.) The speed at which she arrives from observed conditions to the appropriate action relies on how much information she is able to chunk or process at the same time. Chunking or processing several information at the same time can be developed through practice. We'll help you figure out how to practice by figuring out the skills of a DCS operator. But first, you need to drown in a sea of documentation.

Here's what the textbook tells us about DCS.

Distributed control system (DCS) is used to control production systems within the same geographic location. It usually involves a computer that communicates with control elements distributed throughout the plant or process, e.g. machine or process controllers and PLCs, through a bus or directly and displays gathered data (Bolton, 2015).
